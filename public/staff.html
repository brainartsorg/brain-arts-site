Meet all of the people that make BAO happen!


Departments

BAO is proud to implement a horizontal, power sharing structure with five Directors and a consistent pay rate of $20/hr across the organization.

Communications

Messaging, publishing and distribution of all our communicative efforts, internally and externally.

Programming

Actions including events, protests, demonstrations, and public art projects. Placing artists into educational and creative opportunities, including journalism for the Boston Compass Newspaper. Space-making efforts including the Dorchester Art Project studio, store and hourly space rental programs.

People & Culture

Community building efforts including staff, intern and volunteer coordination and management.

Amyas McKnight

Director of Facilities and DAP Sales Associate

Amyas McKnight is Director Of Facilities at Dorchester Art Project and Founder of What's Happening Boston. Through various jobs McKNight discovered his strengths and passion for grassroots organizing while simentabusoely beginning a career in the entertainment industry. In 2020 these callings merged when he started volunteering for Brain Arts Org and quickly proved a valuable asset to a hyper-local, mission-driven organization. As a lifelong Dorchester resident McKnight is passionate about realizing creative potential and bringing money and power back to his community. He manages all the facilities at Dorchester Art Project including the theater and performance space, gallery and exhibition space as well as the store and rental hall.

Devon Austrie

Director of Finance

Devon Austrie, born in Brooklyn, NY, but raised in Boston, is an art lover through and through. As a drummer and working musician, music is his first love. Comedy and math have remained consistent interests as well as new interests such as wellness, spirituality, and metaphysics. When he’s not doing 100 things at once, you can catch Dev performing, gaming, creating, and learning with the people that he loves. He’s been working part-time at BAO as its bookkeeper since September 2020 and as Director of Finance since November 2020. His mission is to create systems and organize the company’s financial information in a way that everyone can understand and find what they need, while continuing to remain legally and financially compliant. He also wants to teach the community topics on financial literacy, while expanding his own knowledge on accounting and finance, as well as teach workshops on the world of drumming.

Julia Schachnik

Jim Grace

Advisory Board Member

Jim Grace is the ED of the A&BC. Previously he was the ED of the Volunteer Lawyers for the Arts of Mass. from 1998 until 2008, when it merged with the A&BC. Jim has extensive experience working with artists and arts organizations in the areas of legacy planning, public art and social practice art projects, copyright, nonprofit incorporation and mergers, and nonprofit boards. Jim was an adjunct professor for BU’s Masters in Arts Admin Program for more than five years where he co-taught a course on Legal Issues in Arts Admin. As a national leader in the field, Jim trains locally and nationally on a variety of board, legal and artist professional development topics. Jim is also a working author and has been involved in the publication of over eight books. Jim serves on the boards of the Brookline Community Foundation and the AIR Institute in Washington, DC. He has served on the board of Philanthropy Massachusetts and the Private Sector Council of AFTA. He is a founding board member of the Arts Services Coalition and the Fort Point Cultural Coalition (FPCC). The FPCC developed Midway Studios -- 89 units of artist live/work space in downtown Boston.
